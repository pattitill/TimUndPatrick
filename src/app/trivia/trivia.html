Freedom of Speech: Scores and Legend

The Freedom of Speech score reflects the level of press freedom in various countries. Based on the World Press Freedom Index (WPFI) compiled by Reporters Without Borders, the scores assess the autonomy of the media and their ability to operate without interference from the government or other entities.

Freedom of Speech Score Legend:

     Good: 85–100 points
     Satisfactory: 70–85 points
     Problematic: 55–70 points
     Difficult: 40–55 points
     Very Serious: <40 points

What is the World Press Freedom Index?

The World Press Freedom Index (WPFI) is an annual ranking of countries based on their press freedom, compiled by Reporters Without Borders since 2002. It assesses the degree of freedom that journalists, news organizations, and netizens have in each country. The index evaluates several factors, including the political, legal, economic, sociocultural contexts, and safety of journalists.

Higher scores indicate greater press freedom, while lower scores highlight significant challenges. The WPFI methodology changed in 2022, focusing on five distinct categories: political context, legal framework, economic constraints, social and cultural pressures, and the safety of journalists.
